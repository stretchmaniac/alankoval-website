Android Apps

Yep, I've done some Android. Two applications, to be precise.

1. ADKalc Widget ADKalc Widget is a simple widget designed to display a varying message on an Android phone via the home screen. If you ever wanted to compute a formula based on the day, your battery life or the month, ADKalc Widget allows you to do so.

For example, if you wanted to know the floor of your battery level multiplied by a random number between zero and one and added to pi, you could enter:
"My number is... " floor( battery_level * random() + pi )


This was my first experience with parsing, that is, converting input in the form of an equation to something a computer can evaluate.

2. Bouncing Bubbles Live Wallpaper Bouncing Bubbles is a (free) live wallpaper for the android home screen. Instead of a picture as the background, this app makes a moving, organic and slightly distracting arrangement of colorful circles that bounce among themselves.

This was the first Android App I made. It was also the first openly available sample of my work. It's a little corny, but overall I'm happy with how it turned out.

You can change an array of settings, including the bubble colors, the speed of the bubbles, and whether you can pop them or not (by touching them).
